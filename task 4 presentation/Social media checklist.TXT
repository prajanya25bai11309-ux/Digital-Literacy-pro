\# Social Media Checklist for Responsible Use

\## Do's

\- Think before you post; consider long-term consequences.

\- Verify information before sharing it.

\- Maintain professionalism, especially on public profiles.

\- Respect others' opinions and engage in healthy discussions.

\- Protect your personal information and privacy settings.

\## Don'ts

\- Do not share sensitive personal details (address, phone number, etc.).

\- Avoid posting offensive, abusive, or hateful content.

\- Do not spread misinformation or unverified news.

\- Avoid engaging in online arguments or toxic debates.

\- Do not post content that could harm your academic or professional reputation.

# _Project Report_

Poor digital communication can lead to serious misunderstandings and consequences. For instance, imagine a student sending a sarcastic message in a group project chat, criticizing a teammate's work. Since tone is hard to interpret in text, the message is taken literally, leading to conflict within the group. The situation escalates, affecting collaboration and ultimately the quality of the project. This could have been avoided if the student had communicated more clearly and respectfully, or chosen a more appropriate medium like a face-to-face conversation. Using constructive language, avoiding sarcasm, and being mindful of tone are essential in digital communication. Clear, respectful, and thoughtful messaging helps prevent unnecessary conflicts and ensures smoother collaboration in academic and professional settings.
